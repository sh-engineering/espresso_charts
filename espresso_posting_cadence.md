## Principles

**Reels drive reach. Carousels drive saves. Substack deepens the relationship.** Each format has one job. The schedule sequences them to maximise each job without competing with itself.

**Never post the same story twice on the same day.** Splitting attention across two posts about the same content halves the engagement signal on each.

**Space Reel and carousel for the same story by at least 48 hours.** The Reel brings new eyes. The carousel two days later converts those new eyes into followers and savers. This is a funnel, not a flood.

**YouTube Shorts is always the same file as the Reel, posted same day, no extra work.**

**Substack posts before the social posts.** Subscribers see it first. Social posts reference it. This trains the audience that Substack is upstream of Instagram, not a copy of it.

---

## Why this shape

**Monday Reel at 09:00** opens the week with reach. The Chart Note at 06:00 means existing subscribers already have something before the Reel hits — they feel like insiders.

**Tuesday Carousel at 09:00** catches everyone who saw the Monday Reel but did not follow yet. The 18:00 Chart Note is a second Substack touchpoint — different chart, different insight, not a repeat.

**Wednesday mirrors Monday** for Story 1. The Thursday newsletter lands the morning after the Wednesday Reel — the audience has already seen the hook, the newsletter is the payoff.

**Friday Reel into Saturday Carousel** is the same funnel as Monday–Tuesday, compressed into two consecutive days. Saturday at 12:00 catches weekend browsing, which has longer dwell time than weekday mornings.

**Sunday Chart Note** is the best chart of the week — not a new story, a highlight. It rewards the weekend Substack reader and gives the algorithm a clean signal going into Monday.

---

## Flexibility

All three stories are fully produced every week. You choose what to post.

- **If a Reel performs well** — hold the carousel an extra day to let the reach window close before posting the same story again.
- **If a carousel performs well** — post an extra Chart Note referencing it and drive Substack signups off the momentum.
- **If a story feels weak** — post as carousel only, skip the Reel. Save the Reel slot for a stronger story the following week.
- **If you miss a day** — the Chart Note is two to four sentences. Never skip it for operational reasons.

## What not to post

- No Reel and carousel for the same story on the same day
- No double-posting the same chart in any 24-hour window on Substack
- No posting after 20:00 — engagement drops sharply for this content type and audience
